## Project header

**Project.** Redesign of Maxime Luet's personal portfolio, a Product Designer
specialized in UI & Design System who codes (React/Tailwind front-end). ~10 years in SaaS.
The site is itself proof of competence: it must win a recruiter in
a 6-second scan AND hold up to craft inspection by a head of design.

**Audience.** Primary: recruiter / talent, often non-designer, scans in
6–10s. Secondary: head of design / senior designer, judges fine craft and
mistakes. (Full profiles in 90 and 91; recap here, not duplication.)

**Business objective.** Land a Product Designer / UI-UX role at a startup,
scale-up, or large company with an internal product team, remote France or hybrid west.
What changes if the project succeeds: qualified outreach and interviews obtained *via*
the portfolio.

## The four axes

### Axis 1 — Tone register: **Conversational**

Voice-over / cinema, first person, clean concrete sentences ("pixels you
could push"). A real person speaks, not a slogan. Rejects Professional (too
cold for an already distinctive voice) and Playful ("no Medium/Substack tone").

### Axis 2 — Aesthetic philosophy: **Editorial Restrained**

Editorial light, generous white, rare and intentional color, one well-tuned type
family (Syne), discreet interactions. Restraint is the signal. Rejects Polished
Standard — which would be exactly the median Framer template the portfolio must
stand apart from (red flag 2).

### Axis 3 — Relation to audience: **Peer** (Authority accents in Method)

Center of gravity: peer-to-peer. Shared vocabulary, honesty (collaborators
credited, precise scope), the reader is a co-thinker — natural when the
second reader is themselves a head of design. Authority accents *localized* in
the Method section, where convictions are stated as named principles. Rejects
Authority as center: without the notoriety of a star, a "I know" tone would ring
hollow and trigger red flag 8 (overclaiming). Rejects Companion: in a portfolio,
the subject is Maxime, not the reader-protagonist.

### Axis 4 — Sensory ambition: **Considered**

The reader notices the craft without craft being the subject. Serves the dual
requirement: scan impact + depth on inspection. Rejects Functional (a feeling is wanted,
not just information delivered) and Resonant (not architecting an emotion — the
eStorie/grief topic could invite it, but that is not the portfolio's angle,
and aiming for Resonant would risk off-topic pathos).

---

## Synthesis

This combination produces a portfolio that speaks like a thoughtful,
composed person (Conversational), on a clear and patient editorial surface that earns
attention by deserving it rather than holding it by force (Editorial Restrained).
The reader is treated as a peer: the work is shown honestly, the trade's vocabulary is
shared, no lecturing — except in Method, where convictions stand as clean principles.
The craft is visible and holds up to inspection, without ever becoming the subject
instead of the work (Considered). In practice: a site that looks like a working document
held with extreme care, whose system rigor reveals itself on second look, and whose
voice carries half the brand.

## Rejection list (what this brief says no to)

- **No monospace / terminal / blueprint as theme.** Saturated 2026 convention,
  template-look, contradicts a UI-craft positioning. Tolerated only as a rare accent
  (a spec label), never as an ambient mood.
- **No dark mode by default.** Editorial light is the deliberate counter-current choice
  that separates from the tech-dark wave.
- **No decorative gradient / glass.** Rare and localized material only, where it *proves*
  something (the Figma → production transition, a live component state). Never as an ambient background.
- **No slogans or generic enthusiasm.** No "crafted / pixel-perfect / passionate /
  delightful experiences" — empty category vocabulary.
- **No trendy jargon in bulk.** "ship", "craft" used sparingly.
- **No team presented as solo.** Precise scope everywhere (red flag 7).
- **No decorative double diamond** or discovery maps that bury the work.
- **Zero mistakes.** Disqualifying for a profile who claims craft.
- **No second type family as theme.** One well-tuned sans; a second typographic
  voice as a rare accent only.
